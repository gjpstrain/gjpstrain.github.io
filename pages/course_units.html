Course Units

COMP1312 Data Science

This first-year computer science course helps students get to grips with statistics and the standard python tools for data analysis, including scipy.stats, pandas, and numpy. My responsibilities were helping to run the weekly lab sessions and help students wherever they needed. For many this was their first foray into data science, so naturally this could be quite daunting.

COMP23111 Fundamentals of Databases

This was my first foray into databases, having come from a non-computer science background. I learnt as I went along however, and found this unit very satisying, both in terms of the logic involved and the databases themselves. This was a real learning-on-the-job experience, and I my willingness to sit down and help a student figure something out (as I was pretty much doing myself) helped them open up.

UCIL20122 AI: Robot Overlord, Replacement, or Colleague?

This is a flagship interdisciplinary unit that bridges gaps between computer science, philosophy, and the social impacts of technology. This past year I delivered a workshop on this unit in response to the increasing popularity of LLMs such as ChatGPT.
