The Effects of Contrast on Correlation Perception in Scatterplots

My first paper was published in March 2023 in the International Journal of Human-Computer Studies with full gold open access. It is the culmination of the first year and a half of work on my PhD. We found that adjusting contrast as a function of residual distance in scatterplots could partially correct for a longstanding correlation underestimation bias. We carried out all experimental work according to the principles of open and reproducible research, including pre-registered hypotheses, publicly available data and code, and dockerisation of the paper making it fully executable. Everything you need to reproduce the experiments and re-generate the paper in the precise computational environment it was written in is included in a GitHub repository. This research was a blast to do and even more fun to write, once I got my head around Docker, Rmarkdown, and the host of templates and additional LaTeX packages needed to write it. You can find the paper here on ScienceDirect.
