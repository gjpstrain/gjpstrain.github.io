Gabriel Strain

Academic Researcher

I am currently coming towards the end of the second year of my PhD in Computer Science at the University of Manchester. Following a false start studying medicine in 2015, I completed a BSc in Psychology in 2020 followed by a MRes in Research in Psychology in 2021. By this point the research I was involved in sat firmly between perceptual psychology and human-computer studies. I received funding from the University of Manchester's Computer Science Department to begin a PhD in 2021. My primary research track investigates how we can exploit aspects of human perception to design better data visualisations.

In addition to this work, I have also completed research in explainable AI, and have experience as a teacher and instructor, both within psychology, computer science, and a flagship interdisciplinary unit currently being taught at the University of Manchester. I am passionate about open and reproducible research. The Instruction page contains details of my experience in this area, including materials I have produced and a list of the units I have taught on.

The Research page contains information about my work and research practices, including links to my publications, presentations, and GitHub profile, which is where I make all my work, including my code, papers, and datasets, permanently and freely available. I use the Blog page for anything unrelated to work.
